You can ask wh-questions: what, which, who, whom, where and when. You can also use how many or how much. You can also make queries with verbs like: tell, give, return, list, find, show, retrieve, display, search and select.

To signal an aggregation function count you can use: how many and number of.

To signal an aggregation function sum you can use: how many (in a numeric field), how much and total.

To signal an aggregation function avg you can use: average, mean and avg.

To signal an aggregation function min you can use: least, smallest, tiniest, shortest, cheapest, nearest, lowest, worst, newest, min and minimum.

To signal an aggregation function max you can use: most, biggest, longest, furthest, highest, tallest, greatest, best, oldest, max and maximum.

To signal a group by clause you can use: by, per, for each and of each.

To signal a having or where clause condition you can use: more than, greater than, less than, equal to, greater than or equal to and less than or equal to.

To signal a time-scale conversionyou can use: daily, monthly and yearly.

You can customize the settings including, changing and modifying reserved words and subsequent words POS (Part-of-Speech) in the GLAMORISE Settings and NaLIR Settings tabs.
