You can ask wh-questions: what, which, who, whom, where or when. You can also use how many or how much. You can also make queries with verbs like: tell, give, return, list, find, show, retrieve, display, search or select.

To indicate an aggregation function count you can use: how many or number of.

To indicate an aggregation function sum you can use: how many (in a numeric field) or number of (in a numeric field), how much, sum or total.

To indicate an aggregation function avg you can use: average, mean or avg.

To indicate an aggregation function min you can use: least, smallest, tiniest, shortest, cheapest, nearest, lowest, worst, newest, first, min or minimum.

To indicate an aggregation function max you can use: most, biggest, largest, longest, furthest, highest, tallest, greatest, best, oldest, last, max or maximum.

To indicate a GROUP BY clause you can use: by, per or each.

To indicate a HAVING or WHERE clause condition you can use: more than, greater than, less than, equal to, greater than or equal to or less than or equal to.

Some subqueries are accepted. To indicate a time-scale conversion you can use: daily, monthly or yearly.

Another type of subquery, to indicate a nested aggregation function like min or max followed by count or sum you can use: least, smallest, tiniest, shortest, cheapest, nearest, lowest, worst, newest, first, min or minimum or most, biggest, largest, longest, furthest, highest, tallest, greatest, best, oldest, last, max or maximum followed by number of, total or summed. E.g.: highest number of
